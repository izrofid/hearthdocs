### The Toku Region
The Ancient China/Japan inspired Toku Region is the setting of Pokemon Hearth. Being set in the distant past compared to mainline pokemon games, a lot of the terminology is borrowed from Pokemon Legends Arceus. For instance, there are no Pokemon Trainers in Toku. The term used instead is Pokemon Wielder.

#### A Living, Breathing World
Hearth features a dynamic internally managed clock instead of the standard Realtime Clock (RTC) used in Pokemon games. This means the in-game time is not dependent on anything outside it. The player can sleep at Pokemon Refuges to pass time. The world of Hearth will react to the in-game clock as NPCs have different schedules. Some NPCs will only appear at night and vice-versa. For instance, Shamisen Players appear in Refuges at night while the School is only open during the day.

#### Gorgeous Custom Assets
Nearly every asset in Hearth is bespoke. Nearly every NPC has a custom mugshot. The maps and tilesets are made from scratch.

### Learnsets
Hearth uses the Pokemon XY Learnsets as a fallback for all pokemon who don't have custom learnsets. The custom learnsets are not yet documented.
